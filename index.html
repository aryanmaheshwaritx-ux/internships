PRJ-001 · Flagship
Drone-deployed wind turbine sensor payload
Aero Robotics (student venture) · 2025–present
Patent pending · 63/883,099

A drone-mounted system that prepares a small section of a composite blade and bonds a sensor in place, removing the need for a rope-access technician on the tower. The hard parts are not flying. They are surface prep at distance, adhesion that survives blade flex, and keeping payload weight low enough that a commercially available platform can still fly the mission cleanly. I led concept, payload design, vendor sourcing, and field deployment.

PRJ-002 · Capstone
Universal drone attachment platform
Aero Robotics (student venture) · 2024–2025
Built & flown

A modular under-frame mount that lets a single drone carry interchangeable payloads without re-balancing the airframe each swap. Iterated 3D-printed prototypes in Fusion 360 with focus on vibration isolation, structural stability, and cross-platform compatibility. The interesting design problem was the connector: rigid enough to handle vibration in flight, loose enough to hot-swap on the bench.
